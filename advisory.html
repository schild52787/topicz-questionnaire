Financial Overview

A1What is Topicz's approximate annual revenue? High Priority
ContextRevenue scale determines which growth investments are proportionate, which platforms make sense, and what the business looks like to a potential partner or acquirer. It also calibrates every ROI projection we build.
A2What is Topicz's approximate gross margin — revenue minus cost of goods? High Priority
ContextGross margin tells us how much is available for operations, growth investment, and profit after the product is sourced. Tobacco distributors typically operate at thin margins (3–8%) where rebates and scan data programs are the primary lever for improving profitability. Knowing your margin helps us assess how much the scan data and rebate opportunity is worth relative to total profitability.
A3How has Topicz's revenue trended over the past 3 years? High Priority
ContextRevenue trajectory is one of the most important inputs in any business assessment. A declining business needs a different strategy than a growing one — and the cause of the decline (volume, price, account losses, category headwinds) determines the right response. Cigarette volumes have declined industry-wide for decades; any business growing despite that headwind has a meaningful competitive advantage worth understanding.
A4Does Topicz carry debt, a credit line, or outside investment?
ContextCapital structure affects how much of the $75K–$200K investment budget is available without outside financing, and whether a credit facility exists to accelerate growth investments if the ROI case is compelling.

Revenue Quality & Customer Concentration

A5What percentage of Topicz's revenue comes from its top 5 retail accounts? High Priority
ContextCustomer concentration is the single largest risk factor in any distribution business. If 50%+ of revenue comes from 5 accounts, losing one is an existential event. It also affects every growth conversation: a business with diversified revenue across 500 accounts is more valuable, more defensible, and better positioned for a B2B portal rollout than one with 10 major accounts who all have direct manufacturer relationships.
A6How many active retail accounts does Topicz serve, and how many are ordering at least monthly? High Priority
ContextTotal accounts and active accounts are different numbers — and the gap between them is often where the first growth opportunity lies. Reactivating dormant accounts through a self-service portal is frequently faster ROI than acquiring new ones.
A7What is the average monthly order value per active account?
ContextAverage order value, combined with order frequency and account count, gives us the B2B portal revenue model. It also determines minimum order thresholds, which affect whether a portal needs payment-at-checkout or net terms.
A8Does Topicz extend credit terms to retail accounts? If so, what are typical terms?
ContextNet 30/60 terms are standard in tobacco distribution but create a working capital drag that online portals can either worsen (if checkout extends terms automatically) or improve (if portal moves accounts to prepay or shorter terms). This affects how we design the portal's payment flow and whether payment processing needs to integrate with an AR/invoicing system.

Products, Suppliers & Margin Mix

A9Which tobacco manufacturers does Topicz carry, and which accounts for the largest share of purchases? High Priority
ContextManufacturer relationships determine rebate and scan data program eligibility. Altria alone has multiple distinct programs (Retail Leaders, scan data, promotional allowances, buy-downs) that can represent meaningful incremental margin. RJ Reynolds and ITG have comparable structures. Knowing which manufacturers — and at what volume — gives us a concrete dollar estimate for uncaptured rebate opportunity.
A10What is the approximate revenue split between cigarettes and convenience / non-tobacco products? High Priority
ContextCigarette volumes decline ~3–5% annually industry-wide (CDC/SAMHSA data). A distributor that is 90%+ cigarettes faces a structural headwind; one that has meaningfully grown its convenience and non-tobacco mix has built a hedge. This split also determines online strategy: convenience and non-tobacco products have far fewer shipping, age verification, and regulatory constraints, making them the most viable DTC e-commerce category in the near term.
A11Does Topicz have exclusive distribution rights in any territory, or does it compete head-to-head with other distributors for the same accounts?
ContextExclusivity is a significant moat and a key value driver. If Topicz has exclusive territory agreements with any manufacturers, that protection is worth quantifying — and worth protecting as the business goes digital. If distribution is competitive, the portal becomes even more important as a differentiation tool: the first distributor in a market to offer 24/7 self-service tends to win account loyalty.

Competitive Position

A12Who are Topicz's 3–5 primary competitors in its distribution territory? High Priority
ContextNamed competitors allow us to assess online presence, service model, and likely response to Topicz launching a portal. If a direct competitor already has a functioning B2B portal, the urgency for Topicz to act increases materially — first-mover advantage in digital ordering tends to be sticky once accounts are on a platform.
A13Why do retail accounts choose Topicz over a competitor? What's the primary reason they stay? High Priority
ContextThis is the most important competitive question. The honest answer — whether it's pricing, relationships, delivery speed, product breadth, or service reliability — tells us what to protect and what to amplify. It also tells us whether the relationship is sticky enough to survive the migration to a self-service model, or whether accounts need a reason to stay that the portal itself has to provide.
A14Has Topicz lost any significant accounts in the past 12 months? If so, why?
ContextAccount losses are the most telling signal about competitive threats or service gaps. A business that hasn't lost an account in 3 years has a different risk profile than one that has lost 10. The reason for losses — price, service, a competitor's new offering, account closure — shapes the defensive elements of the strategy.

Operations & Management

A15How many full-time employees does Topicz have, and what are the key roles?
ContextHeadcount tells us capacity for execution. A 5-person operation building a B2B portal needs a different implementation timeline than a 50-person operation. It also tells us whether there are sales reps whose roles will be affected by the portal — managing that transition is as important as the technology build.
A16Which states does Topicz hold active tobacco distribution licenses in? High Priority
ContextLicense footprint is the immediate online addressable market. Every unlicensed state is a closed door. Expanding licenses in high-population states (TX, FL, CA, NY, PA, IL) before portal launch dramatically increases the available market with minimal incremental cost — typically $500–$5,000 per state, depending on the state's fee structure.
A17What ERP or distribution management system does Topicz use? High Priority
ContextThe ERP system is the integration anchor for everything — the portal, scan data submission, rebate reconciliation, and inventory sync. Tobacco-native systems (DAC ERP, ManageMore) have pre-built integrations that reduce build time by months. A custom or general ERP (Sage, QuickBooks, homegrown) requires custom middleware. The answer changes the portal build scope, timeline, and cost estimate significantly.
A18Does Topicz have account-level pricing — different prices for the same product for different retailers?
ContextAccount-level pricing is the most common technical complication in B2B portal builds. If every account pays the same price, the portal is straightforward. If you have tiered or negotiated pricing per account, the portal must display each account's correct prices after login — which requires a real-time ERP pricing sync. Getting this wrong (accounts seeing incorrect prices at checkout) destroys portal trust on day one.

Strategic Intent & Exit Horizon

A19What is the owner's 5-year vision for Topicz? High Priority
ContextThe 5-year vision is the frame for every recommendation. Building an online channel to maximize a sale in 3 years looks different from building it to run the business better for the next decade. Both are valid — but they lead to different architecture decisions, investment sequencing, and what metrics we optimize for. We need to know which one this is.
A20Has Topicz received any acquisition interest or approaches from other distributors, larger companies, or private equity in the past 3 years?
ContextAcquisition interest is a data point on how the market values Topicz today — and what the floor and ceiling on valuation might be. It also tells us whether the strategic conversation should include "what would make this business more attractive to the right buyer" as an explicit objective of the growth work.
A21If Topicz were to be valued or sold today, what do you believe the business is worth — and how did you arrive at that?
ContextOwner perception of value is often the starting point for a productive valuation conversation — and the gap between perceived value and market value is where advisory work delivers its most direct impact. Tobacco distribution businesses typically trade at 3–5x EBITDA, modified by revenue quality, concentration risk, and growth trajectory. Your answer helps us understand the current baseline and what moves the number.

Risk & Regulatory

A22Are all products Topicz distributes covered by valid FDA permits or pre-market authorization (PMTA) where required?
ContextThe FDA has been aggressively enforcing PMTA requirements for ENDS and newer nicotine products. Distributing a product that lacks a valid FDA authorization exposes Topicz to enforcement action — and makes that product category unavailable online entirely. This is a clean-up item before any product category goes digital.
A23Are there any outstanding legal issues, regulatory inquiries, or compliance matters affecting the business?
ContextPending legal or regulatory matters affect valuation, investor appetite, and sometimes the scope of what's achievable digitally. They also affect the order in which we recommend fixing things — a business under a state regulatory inquiry needs compliance resolved before launching new online channels.
A24What does Topicz see as its single biggest risk to the business over the next 3 years?
ContextHow leadership thinks about risk reveals priorities, blind spots, and what keeps the owner up at night. Common answers include cigarette volume decline, manufacturer direct-to-retail initiatives, regulatory expansion, or key employee departure. The answer shapes how we frame the online growth strategy — growth vs. defense vs. hedge.
A25Is Topicz currently enrolled in any manufacturer scan data or incentive programs? If yes, which ones? High Priority
ContextPhase 1 indicated Topicz is not currently enrolled but is interested. Confirming the status here and identifying which manufacturers are in scope allows us to begin the enrollment process as an immediate action — before the portal is built. Scan data rebates are one of the fastest, lowest-effort revenue recovery opportunities available to tobacco distributors.
A26Is there anything about Topicz's business, competitive situation, or goals that you think we should know — and that hasn't been covered in this questionnaire?
ContextThe best insight in any advisory engagement usually comes from the thing that doesn't fit a checkbox. Use this space for anything: a pending opportunity, a relationship that matters, a constraint on timing, or simply what you'd most want a trusted advisor to understand about this business before making recommendations.
