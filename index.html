Business Structure & Products

Q1
Why we askThe online growth strategy for a B2B distributor (selling to retailers) is completely different from a DTC retailer (selling to consumers). A distributor's biggest win is usually a self-service ordering portal that replaces phone and rep-driven orders. A retailer's biggest win is usually customer acquisition and conversion. Knowing your model determines everything that follows.
Q2
Why we askNot all tobacco products have the same online sales potential. Cigarettes and ENDS (vaping) face the heaviest federal and state restrictions online. Cigars, smokeless tobacco, nicotine pouches, and accessories have significantly more runway for direct-to-consumer digital sales. Knowing your product mix tells us immediately which categories to prioritize and which to route differently.
Q3
Why we askThese two answers are often different ‚Äî and the gap between them is usually where the opportunity lives. Your highest-revenue category may face the most compliance friction online, while a smaller category may be the fastest path to real online growth. Understanding both helps us prioritize correctly rather than defaulting to your biggest product.
Q4
Why we askOnline sales are only legally available in states where you're properly licensed and registered for excise tax. Trying to sell online into unlicensed states is a significant compliance exposure. Knowing your current license footprint tells us the geographic boundary of your immediate online opportunity ‚Äî and where we'd need to expand licensing before you can sell.
Q5
Why we askOTP excise tax is one of the most complex compliance requirements in tobacco ‚Äî rates, filing methods, and product definitions vary by state. Online sales dramatically increase your multi-state tax exposure. If you're not currently set up to handle multi-state OTP compliance, that's a prerequisite infrastructure investment before scaling online, and it needs to be in the roadmap from day one.

Accounting, ERP & Back-Office Systems

Q6
Why we askYour back-office system is the foundation everything else connects to. Generic software like QuickBooks doesn't handle tobacco-specific requirements such as MSA reporting, scan data submission, or OTP excise tax calculations. Industry-specific platforms like DAC ERP or ManageMore do. Knowing what you're on tells us whether your current system can support online growth or whether upgrading it needs to be part of the roadmap.
Q7
Why we askThese are the exact functions where generic accounting software breaks down for tobacco businesses. Each unchecked box is a potential gap that's either costing you money today (missed rebates, manual reporting errors) or will become a barrier when you scale online. This question helps us understand the true state of your back-office infrastructure, not just what software you're running.
Q8
Why we askTobacco manufacturer rebates ‚Äî volume incentives, promotional allowances, and scan data rebates from companies like Altria and RJ Reynolds ‚Äî can represent 2‚Äì5% of total revenue. Most tobacco businesses are leaving at least some of this on the table due to manual tracking, missed claim windows, or incomplete data. Before we talk about growing revenue, we want to make sure you're capturing all the revenue you're already owed.
Q9
Why we askScan data programs ‚Äî where you submit transaction-level POS data electronically to manufacturers like Altria, RJ Reynolds, and Swedish Match ‚Äî unlock significant rebates and promotional pricing that aren't available any other way. Many businesses qualify but aren't enrolled, or are enrolled but not submitting data correctly. This is often one of the fastest, highest-ROI improvements we can make ‚Äî and it becomes directly relevant when you add online sales channels that need to feed into the same data stream.
Q10
Why we askDisconnected systems are the #1 operational failure point when a business tries to scale online. If your e-commerce channel can't see real-time inventory, you'll oversell, create fulfillment errors, and damage customer trust fast. We need to understand whether you have one connected system or separate silos so we can design an online channel that actually works operationally from day one.
3

Compliance, PACT Act & Legal Landscape

Q11
Why we askThe 2021 PACT Act expansion effectively banned USPS delivery of e-cigarettes and vaping products (ENDS) to consumers, and FedEx/UPS quickly followed. This is the single biggest legal constraint on online tobacco sales today. Your answer tells us immediately how much of your product mix is blocked from standard DTC shipping ‚Äî and whether we need to route around it (B2B-only, adult signature carriers, state-by-state mapping) or focus on your unrestricted categories first.
Q12
Why we askThe FDA mandates 21+ age verification for all tobacco and nicotine product sales. A self-attestation checkbox ("I am 21+") is no longer considered sufficient by most state regulators and exposes you to significant fines. The method you use ‚Äî and its strength ‚Äî affects your liability, your ability to advertise, and which payment processors and carriers will work with you. We need to know your current baseline before recommending improvements.
Q13
Why we askTobacco and nicotine products are classified as "high-risk" by nearly every major payment processor. Stripe, PayPal, and Square all explicitly prohibit tobacco sales and will terminate accounts without warning. Many businesses discover this the hard way ‚Äî mid-growth, with no backup. Knowing where you are helps us ensure your payment infrastructure won't be pulled out from under you when you scale, and whether you're paying unnecessarily high rates for a high-risk processor when better options exist.
Q14
Why we askEvery state you can't sell into online is revenue you're leaving behind ‚Äî but selling into an unlicensed state is worse than not selling at all. Geoblocking unlicensed states is a required part of any compliant online tobacco store, and expanding your license footprint is often a fast path to meaningful revenue growth. Your answer helps us map your current reach and identify where licensing expansion has the highest ROI.

Marketing & Customer Acquisition

Q22
Why we askTobacco and nicotine advertising is heavily restricted on every major digital platform. Google Ads prohibits tobacco products. Meta prohibits tobacco and most nicotine products. TikTok bans them outright. This means the standard playbook for online growth ‚Äî paid search, paid social ‚Äî is largely off the table, and your strategy needs to be built around the channels that actually work: SEO, email, trade relationships, and a few compliant paid options. Knowing what you're currently doing tells us whether you've been trying to run ads that will get your account banned.
Q23
Why we askYour existing customer base ‚Äî the retailers or consumers you already sell to ‚Äî is your most valuable and most underutilized growth asset. A CRM is what allows you to market to them digitally, identify which accounts are at risk of churning, and run targeted campaigns to increase order frequency. Without it, digital marketing to your existing base is essentially impossible, and you're forced to always chase new customers instead of deepening relationships with the ones you have.
