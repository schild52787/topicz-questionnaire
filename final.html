Business Overview

Q1–Q5
Q1How does Topicz generate revenue — B2B wholesale to retailers, direct-to-consumer, or both? Key
Why it mattersThis determines every downstream strategic option. B2B wholesale and DTC have almost no overlap in platform requirements, compliance obligations, or growth strategy.
Q2How long has Topicz been operating, and what is the legal entity structure?
Q3How many full-time employees does Topicz have, and what are the key roles?
Why it mattersHeadcount and role structure determine execution capacity and whether a portal launch will require backfilling, retraining, or new hires. It also tells us which sales rep relationships are at risk of disruption when self-service ordering goes live.
Q4Which states does Topicz hold active tobacco distribution licenses in? Key

Financial Profile

Q6–Q9
Q6What is Topicz's approximate annual revenue? Key
Why it mattersRevenue scale calibrates every recommendation — which platforms are proportionate, which investment levels make sense, and what the business looks like to a potential partner or acquirer. A $5M distributor and a $50M distributor have almost entirely different playbooks.
Q7What is Topicz's approximate gross margin — revenue minus cost of goods? Key
Why it mattersTobacco distributors typically operate at 3–8% gross margin where rebates and scan data programs are the primary lever for improving profitability. Your margin tells us how much the scan data and rebate opportunity is worth in real dollars, and whether the investment case for a B2B portal closes at your revenue level.
Q8How has Topicz's revenue trended over the past 3 years? Key
Why it mattersTrajectory matters more than point-in-time revenue. A business declining 10%/year needs a defensive strategy; one growing 10%/year needs an offensive one. The cause — volume, pricing, account gains/losses, category mix — determines the lever to pull.
Q9Does Topicz carry debt, a credit line, or outside investment?

Products & Suppliers

Q10–Q14
Q10Which tobacco manufacturers does Topicz carry, and which is the primary supplier by purchase volume? Key
Why it mattersManufacturer relationships determine scan data and rebate program eligibility. Altria, RJ Reynolds, and ITG each have distinct programs — Retail Leaders, promotional allowances, buy-downs, scan data — that can represent significant incremental margin at scale. This is the starting point for the rebate recovery analysis.
Q11What is the approximate revenue split between cigarettes and convenience / non-tobacco products? Key
Why it mattersCigarette volumes decline ~3–5% annually industry-wide. A business that is 90%+ cigarettes faces a structural headwind; one that has grown its convenience mix has a hedge. This split also determines online strategy — convenience and non-tobacco products face far fewer compliance constraints, making them the most viable near-term e-commerce category.
Q12Does Topicz distribute ENDS or vaping products (e-cigarettes, disposables, pods)?
Why it mattersENDS are subject to PACT Act shipping bans (USPS, FedEx, UPS) and FDA PMTA requirements. Distributing unauthorized ENDS products is a regulatory exposure. This also affects which product categories can go online at all.
Q13Does Topicz have exclusive distribution rights in any territory, or does it compete head-to-head with other distributors for the same accounts?
Q14Are all products Topicz distributes covered by valid FDA permits or pre-market authorization (PMTA) where required?

Customers & Revenue Quality

Q15–Q20
Q15How many accounts does Topicz have on file, and how many are ordering at least monthly? Key
Why it mattersThe gap between total and active accounts is often the first growth opportunity — reactivating dormant accounts through a self-service portal is faster ROI than acquiring new ones, and requires no new marketing spend.
Q16What percentage of Topicz's revenue comes from its top 5 retail accounts? Key
Why it mattersCustomer concentration is the single largest risk factor in any distribution business. If 60%+ of revenue comes from 5 accounts, losing one is a crisis — not an inconvenience. This number directly affects valuation multiples and every growth conversation.
Q17What is the average monthly order value per active retail account?
Why it mattersAverage order value drives the B2B portal revenue model — what minimum order thresholds make sense, whether checkout requires net terms integration, and how quickly a portal pays back its build cost.
Q18How do retail accounts currently place orders with Topicz?
Q19Does Topicz extend credit terms to retail accounts?
Why it mattersNet 30/60 terms are standard in tobacco distribution but create working capital drag. Portal design must handle this correctly — either showing checkout with payment due later (net terms) or requiring prepay. Getting this wrong breaks trust on day one.
Q20What percentage of Topicz's revenue currently comes from online channels vs. phone/rep-driven vs. in-person?

Technology & Systems

Q21–Q26
Q21What ERP or distribution management system does Topicz use? Key
Why it mattersThe ERP system is the integration anchor for everything — the B2B portal, scan data submission, rebate reconciliation, and inventory sync. Tobacco-native systems (DAC ERP, ManageMore) have pre-built integrations that reduce build time significantly. A general or custom ERP requires custom middleware. This answer changes the portal build scope, timeline, and cost estimate.
Q22Does Topicz have account-level pricing — different prices for the same product for different retailers?
Why it mattersAccount-level pricing is the most common technical complication in B2B portal builds. If all accounts pay the same, the portal is straightforward. If pricing is negotiated per account, the portal must display each account's correct prices after login — which requires real-time ERP sync. Showing wrong prices at checkout on day one destroys adoption.
Q23Can Topicz's current system export transaction-level data per retailer — date, SKU, quantity, and price in a structured format?
Why it mattersTransaction-level data is required for scan data submissions to manufacturers. If the system can export it cleanly, scan data enrollment is a near-term action. If not, it requires a middleware or export fix before rebate recovery can begin.
Q24Who handles Topicz's payment processing, and has the business ever been declined, terminated, or warned by a processor? Key
Why it mattersStandard processors (Stripe, PayPal, Square) prohibit tobacco in their terms of service and will terminate accounts — often without warning — when they identify the product category. A termination mid-scale is far more damaging than proactively switching. If Topicz is on a standard processor today, this is the highest-urgency operational fix before going online.
Q25Does Topicz have a website today, and what does it currently do?
Q26Are Topicz's order management and inventory systems connected — or are online and warehouse operations siloed?

Manufacturer Programs & Compliance

Q27–Q29
Q27Which manufacturer scan data or rebate programs is Topicz currently enrolled in? Key
Why it mattersScan data enrollment can yield up to $36,000/year per retail store location in manufacturer rebates (Petrosoft, 2025). For a cigarette distributor with Altria and RJ Reynolds volume, this is often the fastest available revenue recovery — before any online investment. If Topicz is not enrolled, this becomes the first action item regardless of what else we recommend.
Q28How is manufacturer rebate reconciliation currently handled?
Q29Is Topicz collecting and remitting OTP (Other Tobacco Products) excise tax across all states it distributes into?

Competitive Position

Q30–Q33
Q30Who are Topicz's 3–5 primary competitors in its distribution territory? Key
Why it mattersNamed competitors let us assess their online presence, service model, and likely response to Topicz launching a portal. If a direct competitor already has a functioning B2B portal, the urgency to act increases materially — first-mover advantage in digital ordering tends to be sticky once accounts adopt a platform.
Q31Why do retail accounts choose Topicz over a competitor — and why do they stay? Key
Why it mattersThe honest answer tells us what to protect. It also determines whether the account relationship is sticky enough to survive the migration to self-service ordering, or whether the portal itself needs to provide a new reason to stay.

Q33Are any competitors in Topicz's market already running B2B ordering portals for their retail accounts?
